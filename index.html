Leading the Way

Navigating the Chapter to New Frontiers

The chapter president leads the chapter and the contracting community on goals set forth by organizational and industry needs. The president's responsibilities include but are not limited to: presiding over meetings, appointing directors, promoting chapter membership, adhering to the budget, and maintaining effective communication with the contracting community. The wall below honors the past and present leaders of NCMA Tysons.

Wall of Presidents

Take A Look At Our Current and Former Chapter Presidents.

Jean Marceau Lohier

Jean Marceau Lohier, CPCM, CFCM, is currently a Sr. Contracts Manager with AT&T Government Solutions, Inc. (AT&T), a Subject Matter Expect (SME), the President of the Tysons Chapter of the National Contracts Management Association (NCMA), Co-Lead of the Contracts Working Group for the Integrated Program Management Division (IPMD) of the National Defense Industrial Association (NDIA), a member of the School of Business Industry Advisory Council (IAC) of American Public University System (APUS)/American Military University (AMU) (Government Contracting and Acquisition Program) since 2016 and an Instructor Contractor with INL/CAP Training – U.S. Department of State (DoS).

Prior to his current roles, Mr. Lohier held several contracts management positions, including as Contracts General Manager with GTSI Corp. (now Unicom Government, Inc.) and a Sr. Contracts/Grants Administrator with the Academy for Educational Development in Washington, DC. He provides life-cycle contract management and negotiation for all contract types from pre-sales (including strategy and development support) to post-award and contract close-out phases. He has successfully led, trained, coached, and mentored multiple teams and contracts professionals from Government & Industry. He has also helped establish processes and procedures to ensure compliance with contracts and profitability to the organizations. Nationally, he has presented at conferences and has published several professional articles most of them in the Contract Management Magazine.

Mr. Lohier holds two post-bachelor Degrees: one from Georgetown University (GU) in Paralegal Studies and the other from the University of Virginia (UVA) in Procurement and Contracts Management. He is a U.S. President’s Volunteer Award Winner Gold (2016-2014-2013) and Silver (2015-2012). He is the 2015 recipient of the prestigious “NCMA’s Best Contract Management Magazine Article” award.

President From 2016 to 2018

Jim Kuderko

Jim Kuderko is the Senior Corporate Recruiter at British Standards Institution (BSI) in Herndon, VA. He has been in the recruiting field for over 10 years in the greater DC area with emphasis in contracts and procurement, IT, marketing, consulting and ISO Standards.

Jim began his career with AC Nielsen and completed a combined 22 years of service in Marketing and Media Research. He has been a member of NCMA since 2007 and has served NCMA Tysons as Director of Volunteers, VP of Membership, VP of Communications, President - Elect and now President for the 2015 - 2016 Program Year.

At the 2014 Chapter Leadership Summit during World Congress, he was welcomed into the NCMA President’s Circle Club for long-term volunteer service to NCMA.

During the Fall 2014 Chapter Leadership Summit in Washington, DC, he presented as part of the topic Welcome to the Team!, highlighting a new member's experience joining NCMA, and the process of being welcomed into the chapter. At the Summer Chapter Leadership Summit in Nashville, Tennessee in 2013, Jim presented on the topic, What are your Products and Services, which highlighted NCMA Tysons vast array of products and services that are offered to its membership. He was honored with the James E. Cravens Award in 2010, which recognizes outstanding membership accomplishments and highlights achievements in membership recruitment and retention.

Jim graduated from Central Michigan University with a Bachelor's Degree in Journalism.
